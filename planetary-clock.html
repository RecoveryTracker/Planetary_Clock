Planetary Clock

Layout: Top-down geocentric (Tychonic) view. Earth sits at the center, the Moon orbits Earth, the Sun orbits Earth at about a third of the chart radius, and the other planets, asteroids, and Halley's Comet are drawn orbiting the Sun. Every body's direction on screen is its observer-relative azimuth, so the angles match what's overhead.
Hover any body to see its name.
Clock Mode: snaps the date and time to "now" and re-syncs every minute. Date and Time controls are locked while it's on.
Earth position key: The Earth globe shifts vertically from the chart center to show the Sun's relationship to the horizon.
• Fully above center = Midnight Sun / polar day (Sun never sets)
• Fully below center = Polar night (Sun never rises)
• Near center = normal day/night cycle. The exact shift reflects seasonal tilt + latitude.
